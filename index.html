Blog Strategy

HRoes

HR SaaS — SME Market

Strategy Sample

The problem: HRoes had a well-maintained blog with consistent publishing and decent traffic. But most posts sat between 300 and 500 words and were steadily losing ground to competitors producing longer, more comprehensive content.

I audited their full content library and found strong individual posts with no structural connection between them. Without a pillar-cluster architecture, they were generating impressions without building the topical authority that compounds over time.

My strategy recommended expanding five high-potential posts into 1,500–2,000 word pillar guides, building content hubs around employment law and performance management, and introducing client case studies — the one content type their competitors weren't producing.

What I delivered

A 3-phase content expansion plan with editorial calendar, content hub architecture, and detailed expansion examples for two existing posts including word count targets and structural additions.

Content Growth Strategy

Inkthreadable

Print-on-Demand — Ecom

Strategy Sample

The problem: Inkthreadable's blog was functioning as a seasonal noticeboard — holiday closures, stock updates, product announcements. Useful for existing customers. Completely invisible to anyone new searching for what they needed.

The brand had genuine sustainability positioning and wasn't using content to own it. No evergreen strategy, no keyword-led guides, nothing built to attract POD sellers at the start of their research.

I restructured their entire content mix: a phased publishing cadence scaling from 6 to 12 posts per month, split deliberately between evergreen guides, business tips, sustainability content, and company updates — each mapped to a specific audience goal.

What I delivered

A 3-month content calendar with post titles, category breakdown, and phased publishing plan. Projected 30–50% organic traffic growth within 6–12 months based on comparable content programs.

My process

The content audit.
What I look at first.

Before I recommend anything, I need to see what's already there and understand exactly why it isn't working.

01

Traffic vs Conversion Analysis

I pull twelve months of data from Google Analytics and Search Console to find the gap between what's ranking and what's actually converting. High traffic with low conversions is a search intent mismatch. Low traffic with high conversions means you have something worth scaling. Most companies have both and don't realise it.

02

Search Intent Mapping

Every page on your site is either matching what a visitor expected to find or it isn't. I go through your top-performing URLs and map each one to the intent behind the search — informational, navigational, commercial, or transactional. Mismatches here are one of the biggest silent killers of content performance.

03

Competitor Gap Analysis

Using Ahrefs and Semrush, I look at which keywords your top three competitors are ranking for that you aren't. I categorise these by search volume, keyword difficulty, and commercial intent. The gaps with decent volume, realistic difficulty, and clear buyer intent — that's where the real opportunity usually sits.

04

Content Quality Assessment

I look at your existing content against four questions: Is it comprehensive enough to compete? Does it match search intent? Does it give the reader a clear next step? And would someone learn something they couldn't find elsewhere in five minutes? Most content fails at least two of these.

05

Internal Linking Audit

Google uses internal links to understand the structure and priority of your content. Most company blogs have no deliberate internal linking strategy — posts exist as isolated islands. I map out where links are missing and build a structure that points authority toward your most commercially valuable pages.

06

The Recommendation Report

Everything above becomes a prioritised action plan: what to fix, what to expand, what to cut, and what to build from scratch. Not a 40-page document nobody reads — a clear, specific roadmap organised by impact and effort so you know exactly where to start.
